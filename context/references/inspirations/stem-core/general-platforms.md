# Multi-Domain Learning Platforms

Commercial learning platforms that span multiple STEM domains. Mostly benchmarks: this is the floor a Tessarix lesson must clear and the format reader expectations have been calibrated to.

---

### Brilliant.org [seed]

- **URL**: https://brilliant.org
- **What it does**: Subscription learning platform covering math, science, CS, ML, logic. Lesson narrative with embedded interactive problems woven through — every 2-3 paragraphs, a drag-and-drop / fill-in-blank / multiple-choice / click-the-correct-answer interaction. Daily-problem cadence.
- **Interactive pattern**: In-prose widgets (cross-cutting property) + soft locked-prose gating (pattern 1, skippable but flow-encouraging).
- **For Tessarix**: The canonical "interactive lesson" form — proof that prose + inline widgets scales commercially. Their inline-problem pacing (~every 2-3 paragraphs) is a useful benchmark for Tessarix Teach-pillar density. Less innovative on widget shape than newer entrants like the Polo Club tools or Mathigon.

---

### Cartesian.app [seed]

- **URL**: https://cartesian.app
- **What it does**: Visualisation-first drill-down pages for math. Each topic opens with a manipulable visual figure; the prose comes after. Inverse of the textbook structure where prose introduces a figure.
- **Interactive pattern**: In-prose widgets (cross-cutting) + spatial proximity (pattern 6) where the geometry IS the lesson.
- **For Tessarix**: Counterpoint to Brilliant's "prose first, widget second" — Cartesian inverts this. Pattern to consider for Tessarix: when is "visualisation first" the right entry? Likely when the concept is intrinsically geometric (linear algebra, complex analysis, dynamical systems) rather than procedural.

---

### ScienceInteractive [seed]

- **URL**: https://www.scienceinteractive.com/
- **What it does**: Hands-on science kits + interactive labs / simulations for K-12 and college courses. Mix of physical kits sent to students and software simulations covering biology, chemistry, physics, anatomy.
- **Interactive pattern**: Hands-on lab structure + simulation (no single dominant interactive pattern; varies per offering).
- **For Tessarix**: Mostly out-of-scope as a direct inspiration since it's partly a physical-kit company. But the "lab experiment" structure — set up, run, measure, conclude — is a teaching template that translates to digital: an "ML experiment" widget where the reader proposes a hypothesis, configures a model, observes the result, draws the conclusion. The structured-experiment format (vs free play) is a worthwhile pattern.
